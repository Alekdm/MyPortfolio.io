About Me

Hey there! My name is Alex, and I'm a 26-year-old from California. I'm a proud dad, and I've always been fascinated by computers. It's amazing how you can let your imagination run wild and create all sorts of things!

I got my first IT job back in October 2021, and I've been working hard to become a software engineer ever since, all without any formal education. I'm self-taught in HTML, CSS, and Python, and I'd say I'm an intermediate in those three languages.

I have successfully created two websites for small businesses, utilizing my skills in web development and design to create visually appealing and user-friendly websites that helped my clients stand out in their respective markets. On the programming side, I have worked on various Python projects, including a bot that automates the process of getting discounts on Bestbuy's website and exporting them to a server. My expertise in Python extends beyond the basics, as I continue to dive deeper into advanced techniques to build more sophisticated and powerful programs. I am also eager to expand my knowledge in Java and leverage it for my future programming projects. I am committed to staying up-to-date with the latest programming technologies and trends to provide the best solutions to my clients.

One of my greatest strengths as a programmer is my problem-solving skills, and I love working with a team to tackle complex projects. Sure, I've faced challenges along the way, especially in learning everything on my own, but I've found online tutorials and solutions to be really helpful.

Outside of work, I love spending time with my daughter and dog. When I have free time, I like playing video games with my friends or practicing my Python skills.

For anyone who's just starting out in the world of programming and IT, my advice is to always be open to learning and be prepared to use your brain to solve some complex problems! The most important things to me are my family and personal health, and they guide both my work and personal life. Thanks for getting to know me!
